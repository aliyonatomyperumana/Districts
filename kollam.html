History of Kollam

Kollam, formerly known as Quilon, is one of Kerala's oldest ports and trade centers. Famous for its cashew industry and as the southern gateway to Kerala's backwaters, Kollam has been a major trading hub for centuries, connecting merchants from China, Rome, and the Arab world.

Blessed with beaches, lakes, and forests, Kollam offers a unique blend of heritage and natural beauty. It is also the starting point for the renowned backwater journey to Alappuzha, making it a favorite for travelers seeking peace and culture.
