History of Trivandrum

Thiruvananthapuram, commonly known as Trivandrum, is the capital city of Kerala and a hub of culture, politics, and education. The city has a rich heritage that dates back to ancient times, with mentions in Sangam literature. Once the seat of the Travancore Kingdom, Trivandrum flourished under Maharaja Marthanda Varma, becoming an important center of trade, art, and administration.

The iconic Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, one of the richest temples in the world, stands as a symbol of the city’s devotion and grandeur. Today, Trivandrum is a blend of tradition and modernity, housing top institutions like Technopark, one of India’s largest IT parks, and being a gateway to Kerala’s southern beaches and hill stations.
