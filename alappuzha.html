History of Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is famous for its picturesque backwaters, houseboat cruises, and coir industry. Once an important trade center, Alappuzha still holds cultural significance with events like the Nehru Trophy Boat Race attracting visitors from around the world.

The town’s canals, lagoons, and palm-fringed beaches give it the title “Venice of the East.” It’s also the starting point for exploring Kerala’s backwaters, offering a mix of tranquility and cultural heritage.
